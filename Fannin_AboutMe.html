Megan Fannin

Hello! My name is Megan Fannin and I am currently a junior in high school. As long as I can remember, I have always been fascinated with the idea of structures. Everytime I walk inside one, I notice its architectural features, the reinforcements, and its minuet details. Through my observations, I have discovered my love for engineering, which has driven me to take multiple engineering courses offered at my school. I hope that the skills I have learned from these classes can lead me to a graduate program in Civil Engineering and eventually a hands-on job in that field.

Balancing work, ice skating, piano, debate, and school takes a lot of discipline and responsibility. Through each of these activities I have participated in, I have evolved learned character traits that will assist me when I move into the work force. My main areas of work experience have been being a camp counselor and babysitter. As a babysitter, I constantly have to balance being a playmate and rule enforcer. If a child does not listen I have to enforce rules in a new way for them to understand. This has shaped me to become resourceful, pushing me to think quickly and creatively to present an idea or activity in a more engaging way. I am additionally the Treasurer of the Speech and Debate club. This position has taught me to be cooperative, responsible, and thorough. In debate, you must prepare for the topic you will be presenting at least one month in advance. There is a lot of work and time that goes into preparing because you must have deep insight into the topic and be able to supply numerous sources to back up your point. During this process, I also have a debate partner who I must cooperate with in order to be successful. Lastly, I must be thorough in order to prove my point and win the debate. All the traits of resourcefulness, cooperativeness, responsibility, and thoroughness are valuable in civil engineering. It is important to cooperate with others because I will be working in a team of engineers. Also to create new and efficient technology, I need to be resourceful because there will always be constraints I will have to work around.

Through all the extracurriculars I have completed, there are some accomplishments that I am very proud of. I am a part of a synchronized ice skating team, and we have traveled to many nation-wide competitions. In particular, my team has won first place awards on a national level at the ISI World Championship for the past 4 years. Another accomplishment of mine is participating in the COSMOS summer program at UCSD. This is a 4 week summer camp where only 150-200 students are accepted, and I got the opportunity to work with college professors and research analysts in the field of Structural and Geotechnical Engineering. I was able to learn so much about the topics that Structural Engineers encounter in their everyday lives. I was also able to create my own research project, in which I studied and built base isolation systems. Additionally, this past summer I traveled to Ningxia, China for 2 weeks to volunteer in a local village. A group of 5 students and brought and tested different water filtration systems that we could distribute to the villagers. These villagers were in desperate need of better water because they regularly consumed contaminated Yellow River water. This trip taught me so much about leadership and it opened my eyes to the parts of the world that are less fortunate.
