Work Experience

During my time in my engineering class at school, I have been given the opportunity to go on many educational field trips. One trip that stood out to me was when I went to visit the Patriot Jet Team Foundation. The foundation had many fighter jets and simulators that we were allowed to use. The presentation was led by experienced pilots who taught us not only about their time in the sky, but about how engineering disciplines apply to the manufacturing and maneuvering of their airplanes. They went into depth about how lift and drag work on jets and how both the materials and shape of the planes had to be created to insure the smoothest flight. At the end of the field trip, the director also showed us a presentation talking about all the projects created by engineers in the field of aerospace engineering, such as the SpaceX project by Elon Musk. These real-world examples and applications made me realize how important engineering is in everyday life and only boosted my interest in the field. Although I am not particularly interested in the field of aerospace engineering, the lessons we learned at the foundation helped reinforce topics we just learned in class about air movement and free body diagrams. Overall, this field trip was very eye-opening and helpful. Another trip that furthered my interest in engineering was a trip to Stanford and the Computer Science History Museum. This was my first time on the Stanford campus and I fell in love with the layout and research centers they offered. It inspired me to shoot for higher education so that in the future I could end up at a prestigious institution. The Museum was also extremely interesting. I was able to see how computers and intelligent machines have progressed over time. I am so thankful that I was able to have the experience to go on these educational trips.

One of the other amazing opportunities I receive during class is the ability to listen to multiple guest speakers that have shared their job experience in different fields of engineering. My favorite speakers were Dan Cherrier and Natalina Bernardi, who were professional civil engineers. They gave me crucial information about how to select the correct university based on my future career, what to expect when working, and how to get a license to practice. I myself am interested in civil engineering, so this presentation was very helpful. Both of them emphasized how difficult obtaining an engineering degree can be and how important it is to be licensed. This only pushed me to want to try harder in school to reach their level of expertise. They also explained certain aspects of their job, and how they helped build parts of the Bay Bridge and numerous roads. I love the idea of creating new infrastructure for my community and being a part of rewarding projects. Another speaker I found interesting was Maynard Holliday. He worked for the United States Department of Defense, specifically in the field of machine building. He talked to us about how he has been creating new technology that will improve nuclear power plants and change the course of warfare. He showed us examples of how new technology is aiding soldiers so they can carry more supplies and risk less lives because they have automated systems for spying. He really opened my eyes to the ways technology can be used in all aspects of the world and furthered my interest in engineering.

Through the engineering academy, I have also been introduced to a mentor who is currently in the work-force. I am currently working with Kaley Oldani who is an Environmental Engineer at Lawrence Livermore Labs. She has told me all about the work she has done and how it has impacted her life in many positive ways. She has also been giving me such great advice on how to handle stress in high school and how to improve my leadership skills. So far the most important thing I have learned from my mentor is that effort and perserverence are more important than skill. As long as I continue to have a positive attitude and exert my full effort. I can complete any task.
